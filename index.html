a history of d3js

  • created by @mbostock at Stanford
  • Born out of the Protovis project
  • D3: Data Driven Documents - 2011 Paper published in the IEEE Transactions on Visualization & Computer Graphics

d3js - the good

  • Embraces web standards
  • Doesn't abstract you away from the DOM (document object model)
  • Easy to debug
  • Powerful layouts
  • Widely used (directly or indirectly)
  • Tons of examples
  • It's quite cool

d3js - the "bad"

  • Steep learning curve
  • The data joins will hurt your brain
    • Most important, and most misunderstood concept in D3
    • If it feels like you're doing something wrong, it's wrong

      • Thinking with Joins
